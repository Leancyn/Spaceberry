Modul 4: Double Negatives

A. Objectives

After studying this material, students are able to:

  • Understand the meaning and structure of double negatives

B. Material Description

Double negatives occur when two negative words are used in a sentence, often resulting in a positive meaning.

Types of Double Negatives

  • Negative + negative prefix: e.g., “I didn’t disrespect you” = I respected you
  • Neither or not...either: e.g., “The shop won’t open tomorrow, and neither the restaurant” = Both won’t open
  • Two negative verbs: e.g., “She doesn’t bring the ATM, so she cannot withdraw”

Example

Man: I’d like to return this sweater because it’s too small. I don’t have the receipt though.
Woman: You could exchange the sweater for another size. But if you don’t have the receipt, I won’t be able to give your money back.

Answer: A. He can only get a refund with a receipt.

Exercise 4.1

Listen to the short dialogues and identify the use of double negatives. Underline the expressions and choose the best interpretation of each statement.

C. Review Exercises

Review the concepts of double negatives in real conversations. Pay attention to double negative markers and their implied meanings.
